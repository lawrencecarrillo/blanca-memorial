Her Life
Blanca Estella Galvan was born on September 1, 1957 in Delano, California. She spent her life rooted in her community, her faith, and above all, her family.

For 25 years, Blanca served the students of Delano High School as a food service worker. Day after day, she showed up with her signature smile, making students feel seen and cared for. Generations of young people passed through her line, and many carry a warm memory of her kindness.

Blanca was funny, radiant, and full of warmth. Her smile was something people noticed and never forgot. She had a rare and beautiful gift — the ability to walk into a room and make every single person in it feel comfortable, welcome, and loved. Her home was always open. Her heart was always full.

She loved her children fiercely. She delighted in her grandchildren and great-grandchildren. She cherished family time, trips to Las Vegas, and losing herself in a good book. She lived simply and generously, and she leaves behind a community made immeasurably better by her presence.

Blanca had wonderful taste and a spirit that could hold it all at once. She loved Barry Manilow, Earth Wind and Fire, and Garth Brooks. She found magic in The Wizard of Oz and never tired of It’s a Wonderful Life. And in true Blanca fashion, she also adored The Birdcage, To Wong Foo, Thanks for Everything! Julie Newmar, and Friday — because she loved to laugh, she loved people, and she never took herself too seriously. That was her gift.
